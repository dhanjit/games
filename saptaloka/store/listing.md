## Full description (max 4000)

```
Ascend the seven worlds. Swipe your dharma.

Saptaloka is a mythology rogue-like for the phone. Each card is an encounter —
a god, an asura, a trial. Swipe left or right to choose, and watch your four
virtues shift with every decision.

Keep all four in balance between 0 and 100:
• Prāṇa — your life force
• Tejas — divine radiance
• Karma — the weight of your deeds
• Bhakti — devotion

Let any of them collapse and the run ends. But beware the false summits, too:
max out Karma and you are lifted to Svarga; max out Bhakti and you are crowned
a Deva — yet both are heavens still turning inside saṃsāra. The run ends, but
it is not liberation.

The only true win is climbing all seven lokas — Bhūloka, Bhuvarloka, Svarloka,
Maharloka, Janaloka, Tapoloka, and finally Satyaloka, the realm of truth. That
is mokṣa.

Each realm ends with a boss: a major asura or trial you must pass to ascend.
Every way a run can end — five deaths, two false summits, one liberation — has
its own ending.

When a run ends you keep the Puṇya you earned. Spend it in the Mirror of Maya
on permanent upgrades that carry into future runs and make the long climb
survivable.

• Thumb-only controls — swipe, inspired by Reigns
• Rogue-like runs with meta-progression, inspired by Hades
• Works fully offline once installed
• No accounts, no ads, no tracking — collects nothing

How far can your dharma carry you?
```
